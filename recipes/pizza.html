Pizza

This simple brick-oven pizza recipe has been made famous by several well-known, Brooklyn wood-fire pizzerias. Best accompanied by ice-cold, pilsner-style lager beer.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough.
  • 6 ounces low moisture mozzarella cheese, thinly sliced.
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano.
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper.
  • ½ cup no salt added canned crushed tomatoes.
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil.
  • 6 leaves fresh basil, torn.

Steps

  1. Lightly dust one dough ball with flour and stretch gradually until about 14 inches in diamete.
  2. Lay 1/2 of the mozzarella on the crust.
  3. Sprinkle 1/4 teaspoon oregano and 1/8 teaspoon pepper over the top.
  4. Randomly arrange 1/4 cup crushed tomatoes over the pizza.
  5. Drizzle 1 tablespoon olive oil over top.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until the crust begins to brown, 4 to 6 minutes.
  7. Slide the pizza onto the peel and remove from the oven.
  8. Sprinkle 1/2 of the basil leaves randomly over the pizza.
  9. Cut into wedges and serve.
